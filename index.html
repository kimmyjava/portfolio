To Whom It May Concern,

I'm Kimberly Java, an AI automation specialist with hands-on experience building practical, production-ready workflows that solve real business problems. I don't just talk about AI — I build with it.

My most recent project is a WhatsApp AI Customer Service Bot with RAG built on n8n, greenAPI, Groq AI, Pinecone, and Cohere. The bot receives incoming WhatsApp messages, searches a vector knowledge base for accurate product and policy information, generates intelligent context-aware replies, remembers conversation history per customer, and automatically escalates complex complaints to a human agent — all in real time.

Screening Task Response

Business Problem
& AI Solution

1. Business Problem
E-commerce customer service teams struggle to respond to WhatsApp inquiries quickly and consistently. Generic AI bots hallucinate product details, leading to customer distrust — especially around pricing and product availability.

2. How I Used AI to Improve It
I built a WhatsApp customer service bot with RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation). The bot searches a Pinecone vector database of real store data before every reply, ensuring accurate answers grounded in actual product info — not AI guesswork.

3. Tools Used
n8n — workflow automation · Groq API — AI responses · greenAPI — WhatsApp integration · Pinecone — vector database · Cohere — text embeddings · Simple Memory — conversation history

4. Workflow
Incoming WhatsApp message → Extract customer message → AI Agent searches Pinecone knowledge base → Generates grounded reply → IF human flag set → route to human → ELSE → send reply via WhatsApp.

5. Risk & Mitigation
The AI could still occasionally give incomplete answers if the knowledge base lacks coverage. To handle this, the system is instructed to say "I don't have information about that" and escalate to a human agent — rather than guess or hallucinate. The knowledge base can be updated at any time by upserting new records to Pinecone.
